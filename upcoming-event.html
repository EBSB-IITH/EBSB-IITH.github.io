Marble Pool

6:30-9:00 PM
OAT

×
  • Breaking: Decide who will break. The player breaking must aim to strike the racked balls with the cue ball, attempting to pocket one or more balls on the break.
  • Determining Solids or Stripes: After the break, if a player pockets a ball, they are assigned that group of balls (solids or stripes) for the rest of the game. If no balls are pocketed on the break or if both groups are pocketed, the player continues until they pocket a ball of their designated group or commit a foul.
  • Turns: Players take turns shooting, aiming to pocket their assigned group of balls. The cue ball must first contact one of the player's designated balls, and they must call both the ball and the pocket they intend to pocket it in.
  • Pocketing Balls: Players continue shooting as long as they legally pocket one of their designated balls on each shot. If a player pockets an opponent's ball, it remains pocketed, but their turn ends, and the opponent gets to shoot next.
  • 8-Ball: Once a player has pocketed all of their designated balls, they must then pocket the 8-ball to win the game. Like other shots, the player must call the pocket for the 8-ball. However, if the player pockets the 8-ball before all their designated balls are pocketed, they lose the game.
  • Fouls and Loss of Turn: If a player commits a foul (e.g., scratches, fails to hit their own designated ball first, or pockets the cue ball), their turn ends, and their opponent gets to place the cue ball anywhere on the table for their next shot (known as "ball in hand").
  • Winning: The player who legally pockets all of their designated balls and the 8-ball in a called pocket wins the game. Conversely, if a player pockets the 8-ball prematurely or commits a foul while attempting to pocket it, they lose the game.

Stick Relay

6:30-9:00 PM
OAT

×
  • Participants stand in a circle formation, facing inward, with enough space between them to pass the stick overhead.
  • One participant starts the game by holding the stick overhead.The game begins by playing a song. As the song continues , the participant holding the stick passes it overhead to the person next to them in the circle.
  • Each participant must receive the stick from the previous person and pass it to the next without dropping it.
  • If a participant drops the stick during the game, then he is out.
  • As the number of participants get reducing , there is more gap between them.
  • The last participant standing till the end wins the game.
